The Japanese giant salamander (Andrias japonicus) is one of the largest amphibians in the world, reaching lengths of up to 1.5 meters. These remarkable creatures have remained virtually unchanged for millions of years, earning them the title of "living fossils". Found exclusively in cold, fast-flowing streams in Japan, these salamanders are fully aquatic and possess wrinkled skin that increases surface area for oxygen absorption.
